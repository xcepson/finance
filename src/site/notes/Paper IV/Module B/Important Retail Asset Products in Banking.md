In retail banking, retail asset products represent the loan portfolio extended to individual consumers to fund personal, housing, educational, and mobility needs. These products build long-term customer relationships and generate sustainable interest income for financial institutions.

1. Home Loans

A home loan (or mortgage loan) is a long-term secured loan offered to individuals to purchase, construct, or extend a residential property.

·        **Underlying Security:** The residential property serves as collateral through an equitable or registered mortgage. The bank retains original property title deeds until full repayment.

·        **Tenure & Loan-to-Value (LTV):** Tenures extend up to 30 years. Banks fund between 75% to 90% of the property’s market value (LTV ratio), requiring the borrower to fund the remaining portion as down payment (margin money).

·        **Interest Structure:** Offered at floating rates (tied to external benchmarks like the RBI Repo Rate / EBLR) or fixed rates.

·        **Tax Benefits:** In India, borrowers can claim tax deductions under Section 80C (principal repayment) and Section 24(b) (interest payment) of the Income Tax Act.

2. Housing Loan under Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY)

PMAY is a flagship housing mission by the Government of India designed to address urban and rural housing shortages by providing financial credit-linked subsidies on home loans.

·        **Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS / ISS):** Under PMAY schemes (such as PMAY-U 2.0), an interest subsidy of 4% is provided on loan amounts up to ₹8 Lakhs for eligible beneficiaries.

·        **Maximum Benefit:** A maximum interest subsidy of up to ₹1.80 Lakh is directly credited upfront to the borrower's loan account, reducing the net outstanding principal and monthly EMI burden.

·        **Target Income Segments:** Economically Weaker Sections (EWS - annual income up to ₹3 Lakh), Low Income Groups (LIG - ₹3 Lakh to ₹6 Lakh), and Middle Income Groups (MIG - ₹6 Lakh to ₹9 Lakh).

·        **Key Condition:** The beneficiary family must not own a permanent (pucca) house anywhere in India, and the property title generally mandates ownership or co-ownership by a female family member.

3. Home Improvement Loans

Home improvement loans are specialized credit facilities disbursed to existing or new homeowners to fund structural modifications, repairs, or upgrades to an existing property.

·        **Scope of Work:** Civil structural repairs, flooring replacement, painting, roofing work, plumbing, and electrical overhauls.

·        **Loan Structure:** Secured against the existing property, offering lower interest rates compared to unsecured personal loans, with flexible tenures ranging from 5 to 15 years.

4. Home Decor Loans

Home decor loans cater specifically to interior aesthetic enhancements rather than major structural alterations.

·        **Scope of Funding:** Interior designer fees, modular kitchens, custom carpentry, loose/fixed furniture, lighting fixtures, wallpapers, and smart home automation.

·        **Product Comparison:** Unlike Home Improvement Loans which require technical site inspections and engineer estimates, Home Decor Loans are typically processed as unsecured personal loans or top-up home loans based on vendor quotations.

|   |   |   |
|---|---|---|
|**Parameter**|**Home Improvement Loan**|**Home Decor Loan**|
|Primary Focus|Structural repairs, additions, civil works|Interior aesthetics, furnishings, modular fittings|
|Collateral Basis|Secured against the property|Unsecured credit or top-up home loan|
|Valuation Requirement|Engineer estimate / site valuation|Vendor quotations / invoices|

5. Auto / Vehicle Loans

Auto loans are secured retail loans used to finance the purchase of new or pre-owned vehicles (four-wheelers, two-wheelers, and commercial retail transport).

·        **Hypothecation:** The purchased vehicle is hypothecated in favor of the lending bank until full repayment. The bank's lien is endorsed on the Vehicle Registration Certificate (RC).

·        **LTV & Tenure:** Banks finance 80% to 100% of the vehicle's ex-showroom or on-road price, with repayment tenures ranging between 3 to 7 years.

·        **Repossession Rights:** In cases of default, the bank holds legal authority to repossess and auction the hypothecated vehicle to liquidate outstanding dues.

6. Personal Loans

Personal loans are unsecured retail assets granted without collateral, evaluated purely on the applicant’s income stability, employer profile, and credit score.

·        **End-Use Flexibility:** No end-use restrictions—funds can be used for medical emergencies, weddings, travel, education, or debt consolidation.

·        **Tenure & Risk Pricing:** Short-to-medium repayment periods (1 to 5 years) with higher interest rates to compensate for unsecured credit risk.

·        **Digital Processing:** Frequently issued as instant pre-approved loans via digital channels.

7. Educational Loans

Educational loans are social-retail assets designed to finance higher education for students pursuing technical, professional, or vocational studies in India or abroad.

·        **Covered Expenses:** Tuition fees, examination fees, hostel charges, books, equipment (laptops), and international travel tickets.

·        **Moratorium Period (Grace Period):** Repayment does not begin immediately. Borrowers get a moratorium covering the Course Duration + 6 to 12 months, during which simple interest accrues.

·        **Co-applicants & Collateral:** Parents/guardians act as co-applicants. Loans up to ₹4 Lakh to ₹7.5 Lakh require no third-party collateral (under credit guarantee frameworks like NCGTC), whereas higher amounts require tangible collateral.

8. Other Retail Loans

Banks offer several specialized credit lines to cater to targeted retail needs:

·        **Gold Loans:** Fast-disbursing loans granted by pledging gold jewelry/coins as collateral (high LTV, short tenure).

·        **Loan Against Property (LAP):** Multi-purpose secured credit granted by mortgaging a fully constructed, debt-free commercial or residential property.

·        **Loan Against Securities (LAS):** Overdraft facility secured against equity shares, mutual fund units, or life insurance policies.

·        **Consumer Durable Loans:** Point-of-sale micro-financing for purchasing electronics and appliances (often structured as 0% interest EMIs).

·        **Plot / Land Purchase Loans:** Financing provided strictly for purchasing non-agricultural land intended for future residential construction.

9. Processing of Retail Loans

The retail loan lifecycle follows a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) from initial sourcing to final account closure:

·        **1. Application & Document Sourcing:** Customer submits the completed loan application form along with KYC proofs, income documents (salary slips, Form 16, ITRs), bank statements, and property/asset records.

·        **2. Credit Appraisal & Underwriting:** Credit officers pull credit reports (e.g., CIBIL score) and calculate eligibility metrics including Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio (FOIR) and Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio.

·        **3. Field Verification, Technical & Legal Checks:** Includes Contact Point Verification (CPV) for address/employment, Legal Opinion from independent advocates for title deeds, and Technical Valuation by empanelled engineers.

·        **4. Sanction & Terms Offer:** The bank issues a formal Sanction Letter detailing loan amount, interest rate, tenure, EMI, processing fees, and pre-disbursement conditions.

·        **5. Documentation & Disbursement:** Execution of loan agreements, creation of mortgage/hypothecation, submission of NACH/e-Mandates, and disbursement of funds to the seller, builder, or borrower.

**6. Post-Disbursement Monitoring & Closure:** Tracking EMI repayments, maintaining collateral safety, updating bureau records, and issuing No Objection Certificates (NOC) upon final loan closure.
